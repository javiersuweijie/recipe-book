---
layout: recipe
title: Bo-ssam Pork
description: Tender Korean boiled pork belly simmered with onion, ginger, garlic, doenjang, brown sugar, and hazelnut coffee for a rich, savory flavor.
servings: 6
prep_time: 15 minutes
cook_time: 1 hour 15 minutes
resting_time: 10 minutes
ingredients:
  - 3 pounds [1.36 kg] pork belly (or pork shoulder), rinsed and drained
  - 1 large onion [about 300 g], cut into quarters or sliced
  - 2 tablespoons [12 g] ginger, sliced thinly
  - 12 garlic cloves [about 36 g]
  - 2 tablespoons [30 g] Korean fermented soybean paste (doenjang)
  - 1 tablespoon [12 g] brown sugar
  - 1 tablespoon [5 g] instant hazelnut-flavored coffee
  - 10 cups [2.4 L] water
notes:
  - The hazelnut coffee gives the pork its signature color and a deeper savory aroma.
  - Let the pork cool before slicing so it stays neat and juicy.
  - Serve with cabbage, lettuce, kimchi, or rice if you want a simple meal.
---

1. Add the pork, onion, ginger, garlic, doenjang, brown sugar, instant hazelnut coffee, and water to a large pot. Cover and cook for 1 hour over medium-high heat.

2. When the pot comes to a vigorous boil, turn the pork over with a wooden spoon or tongs.

3. After 1 hour of boiling, turn the heat down to low and cook for another 15 minutes.

4. Remove the cooked pork and let it cool for 10 minutes before serving or slicing.
